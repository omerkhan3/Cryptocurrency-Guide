FAQ

What is Cryptocurrency?

Cryptocurrency is a form of digital currency that is designed to be secure and, in most cases, anonymous.

What are some examples of cryptocurrency?

Some of the most popular currencies include Bitcoin, Litecoin, Ethereum, Monero, IOTA, and Zcash.

What's the hype around bitcoin?

Bitcoin is a decentralized, peer-to-peer, "cryptocurrency" system designed to allow online users to process transactions through digital units of exchange called Bitcoins. Started in 2009 by a mysterious programmer, Bitcoin has generated plenty of interest and controversy as a "third" type of currency and an alternative to government flat currencies like the U.S. dollar or the euro or pure commodity currencies like gold or silver coins.

Is Ethereum similar to Bitcoin?

Kind of, but not really. Like Bitcoin, Ethereum is a distributed public blockchain network. Although there are some significant technical differences between the two, the most important distinction to note is that Bitcoin and Ethereum differ substantially in purpose and capability. Bitcoin offers one particular application of blockchain technology, a peer to peer electronic cash system that enables online Bitcoin payments. While the Bitcoin blockchain is used to track ownership of digital currency (bitcoins), the Ethereum blockchain focuses on running the programming code of any decentralized application.

Is it too late for me to invest?

If you believe in the platform, no it's not. You may have heard the word "bubble" thrown around. And yes, as is with most investments, there is inherent risk involved. However, if you believe in the long-term mainstream adoption of crypto, then there is still a lot of potential. The days of making millions of Bitcoin may have passed, but for the cautious investor, it may still represent opportunity.
